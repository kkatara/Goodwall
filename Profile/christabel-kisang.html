About Christabel Kisang

Christabel Kisang is a passionate climate activist and Goodwall ambassador from Kenya, dedicated to bridging the gap between technology and environmental sustainability. With a deep commitment to youth empowerment, she leverages innovative digital solutions to address the urgent climate crisis while creating inclusive opportunities for young Africans.

As a vocal advocate for climate action, Christabel believes that technology should be a tool for social and environmental good. She works tirelessly to ensure that young people, particularly in underserved communities, have access to the resources, knowledge, and platforms needed to become agents of change in their communities.

Her Mission & Vision

🌍 Climate Crisis

To accelerate the transition to sustainable practices through technology-driven solutions that reduce carbon footprint and promote environmental conservation across East Africa.

👥 Youth Inclusion

To empower young people with digital literacy and leadership skills, creating pathways for them to participate meaningfully in climate action and community development.

💻 Technology for Good

To democratize access to technology and innovation, ensuring that underserved communities can leverage digital tools to solve local environmental and social challenges.

Her Impact & Achievements

Creating Real Change

Through her work as a Goodwall ambassador, Christabel has successfully mobilized over 500 youth across Kenya to actively participate in climate action initiatives. Her technology-driven approach has made environmental sustainability accessible and engaging for young people who might otherwise feel disconnected from these global issues.

Her initiatives have directly contributed to:

  • 500+ youth empowered with digital and climate literacy skills
  • 15+ climate projects launched addressing local environmental challenges
  • 20+ communities reached with sustainable technology solutions
  • 80% success rate in youth climate project completion
  • 10,000+ tons of CO2 offset through her community initiatives
  • 100+ women trained in climate technology and entrepreneurship
